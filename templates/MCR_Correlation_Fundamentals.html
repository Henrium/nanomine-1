What are correlation functions?

They are statistical descriptors that quantitatively connect the macroscopic properties of a heterogenous image to its complex microstructure. Mathematically, they quantify the conditional probability of finding two randomly chosen points in the same phase (autocorrelation).

What are the different types of correlation functions available in Nanomine?

Nanomine offers four most commonly used two-point correlation functions: (Refer schematic for graphical explanantion*)

1) Two Point Autocorrelation(S2(r)) - Probability that two randomly chosen points, seperated by distance 'r', fall in the same phase.

2) Two Point Lineal Path Correlation(L(r)) - Probability that a randomly selected line segment of length 'r' falls entirely in the same phase.

3) Two Point Cluster Correlation(C2(r)) - Probability that two randomly selected points, seperated by distance 'r', fall in the same cluster.

4) Two Point Surface-Surface Correlation(FS(r)) - Probability that two randomly selected points, seperated by distance 'r', fall into a dilated region associated with the surface of one phase in the limit of vanishingly small dilation.


*Adapted from Jiao et.al. (see references).
